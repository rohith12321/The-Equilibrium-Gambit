# The Equilibrium Gambit

*A project-driven exploration of quantitative finance, probability, Bayesian inference, and game theory through progressively complex computational simulations.*

---

## Overview

**The Equilibrium Gambit** is a collection of simulation-driven projects that explores how intelligent agents make optimal decisions under uncertainty.

Beginning with probabilistic modeling and stochastic simulation, progressing through Bayesian reasoning and strategic information games, and culminating in an adversarial trading simulation, each module introduces a new mathematical framework that becomes a building block for the next.

The journey concludes with **Operation Dark Pool**, where these concepts are combined to design an autonomous execution agent capable of executing institutional orders in an adversarial financial market.

## Projects

### 01 · Probability and Simulation

Explore how mathematically grounded decision-making outperforms heuristic strategies in dynamic stochastic environments through large-scale simulations.

**Concepts**

- Expected Value
- Kelly Criterion
- Monte Carlo Simulation
- Wealth Optimization
- Dynamic Probability Models

---

### 02 · Bayesian Games

Study strategic decision-making under incomplete information through competitive simulations involving uncertainty, signaling, deception, and Bayesian reasoning.

**Concepts**

- Bayesian Inference
- Posterior Belief Updates
- Bluffing & Signaling
- Mixed Strategies
- Sequential Decision Making

---

### 03 · Operation Dark Pool

Design an autonomous execution agent for institutional order liquidation in an adversarial market.

The agent combines Bayesian belief updates with mixed-strategy Nash equilibria to minimize market impact and execution slippage while adapting to a predatory market maker.

**Concepts**

- Bayesian Belief Updates
- Mixed-Strategy Nash Equilibria
- Market Impact Models
- Monte Carlo Evaluation
- Adaptive Execution Policies

---

## Highlights

- Implemented **Bayesian inference** for adaptive decision-making under uncertainty.
- Developed **mixed-strategy Nash equilibrium** based execution policies for adversarial financial environments.
- Built a **Monte Carlo simulation framework** for evaluating intelligent trading strategies.
